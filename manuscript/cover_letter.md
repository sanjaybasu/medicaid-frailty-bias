Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Redesigning Medicaid Frailty Algorithms: Improved Identification of Medically Frail Adults Under Community Engagement Requirements," for consideration as an original research article in *Health Affairs*.

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 (Public Law 119-21) mandates that all states implement Medicaid community engagement requirements with medically frail exemptions by January 2027. Yet no prior study has systematically evaluated whether existing state frailty algorithms adequately identify adults with functional disability, or specified an evidence-based redesign.

Using individual-level data from 75,043 Medicaid-enrolled adults across 17 states, we find that existing frailty algorithms identify only 31.4% of adults with functional disability as medically frail—missing more than two-thirds of the target population. Identification is lowest among American Indian/Alaska Native (27.5%), Black (26.2%), and Hispanic (28.9%) enrollees, and 3–5 percentage points lower in nonmetropolitan areas within racial groups. An evidence-based redesigned algorithm incorporating expanded diagnostic criteria, health information exchange integration, ex parte determination, and elimination of physician certification raises mean sensitivity to 45.6%, with gains in all 17 states. The redesigned algorithm narrows the AIAN-White identification gap by 46% and the Black-White gap by 10%, while identifying an estimated 3.8 million additional medically frail adults and averting approximately 253,000 coverage losses.

We believe this manuscript is well-suited for *Health Affairs* for several reasons:

**Immediate policy relevance.** States face a January 2027 deadline to implement community engagement requirements. CMS retains authority to establish minimum algorithmic design standards, and our findings provide an empirical basis for such standards—quantifying the 30 percentage point gap between the least and most inclusive existing algorithms.

**Novel contribution.** Prior research has documented racial unfairness in health algorithms and coverage losses under community engagement requirements, but no study has connected algorithmic design to identification sensitivity or proposed a specific, evidence-based redesign. Our three-channel microsimulation framework—incorporating algorithm design, claims visibility, and documentation burden—provides a structured approach for evaluating frailty determination systems.

**Multi-dimensional equity analysis.** We move beyond Black-White comparisons to evaluate disparities across four racial/ethnic groups and rural-urban geography simultaneously, revealing that algorithmic redesign produces heterogeneous equity effects: large AIAN-White gap reductions, modest Black-White improvements, and persistent within-race rural-urban gaps that warrant complementary interventions.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration at another journal. All analysis code is available at https://github.com/sanjaybasu/medicaid-frailty-bias.
